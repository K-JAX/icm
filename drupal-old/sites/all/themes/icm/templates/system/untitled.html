Gifts

  • You are prohibited from giving anything of value, including gratuities, in excess of one hundred dollars ($100) per individual per year to any client, principal, proprietor, employee, agent or representative of another person where such payment or gratuity is in relation to the business of the recipient's employer.
  • You are REQUIRED to submit a request to Compliance through Schwab Compliance Technologies (https://client.schwabct.com/login.do) PRIOR to giving any gifts/gratuities. If you have any questions about your responsibilities regarding the giving of gifts you should discuss them with your Supervising Principal or Compliance.

Entertainment

 

For the purposes of business entertainment, approval is required for all business entertainment expenses

exceeding $100, and the following policies apply:

 

  • entertainment should not be so lavish or frequent in nature that a customer or an employee would likely feel compelled to act in a manner that is inconsistent with theirs or their employers interests;
  • transportation to and from an event is permissible as long as such forms of transportation are reasonable in nature (i.e. not renting a helicopter, personal jets, etc.)

 

Keep in Mind:

Associated persons are required to report gifts, gratuities and entertainment to the firm by completing the Gift/Entertainment Request Form which requests details of the arrangement. If gifts are given to multiple recipients by an associated person, the associated person is required to report each recipient and calculate the value of the gift on a pro rata per recipient basis. A gift basket valued at $250 delivered to an office of three individuals for the benefit of each individual would be permitted.

Our CE Principal will oversee the maintenance of our gifts and gratuities log, reviewing it periodically, looking for any patterns that may raise concern. Any gift or gratuity received by an employee or an immediate family member that does not clearly fall within the above exceptions for reporting must be reported to our CCO. We will maintain copies of all requests along with the any responses.

Selling away is prohibited

  • Selling Away is when a broker solicits you to purchase securities not held or offered by the brokerage firm. As a general rule, such activities are a violation of securities regulations.
  • Typically, when a broker is "selling away," the investments are in the form of private placements or other non-public investments.
  • As a registered representative of NLD you are not permitted to engage in any activity that may constitute “selling away”. Investments such as Private Placements or Private Securities Transactions are not allowed.
